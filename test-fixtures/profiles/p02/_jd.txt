Job Description: Junior Software Developer

Company: NorthBay Logistics Technologies
Location: Hybrid — Tampa, FL (3 days in office, 2 remote)

About Us
NorthBay Logistics Technologies builds routing and dispatch software for mid-sized freight carriers across the Southeast. Our platform moves more than 40,000 shipments a month for customers ranging from 12-truck regional carriers to publicly traded 3PLs. We are a 38-person engineering and product team that ships continuously, keeps a flat structure, and invests heavily in early-career hiring because we believe in growing talent rather than buying it. Last year we promoted four junior engineers to mid-level and rotated two through our platform, mobile, and data teams.

The Role
We are hiring a Junior Software Developer to join our Routing Engine team. You will work alongside two senior engineers and a mid-level developer building the optimization services that assign loads to drivers in real time. This is an entry-level role; we expect to invest 6–12 months in structured onboarding, pair programming, and guided code review before you own features independently.

What You Will Do
- Write and maintain Java services that consume and produce JSON over REST, with guidance from senior engineers
- Contribute to our PostgreSQL data layer by writing queries, reviewing schema changes, and adding indexes under supervision
- Participate in daily standups, sprint planning, and retrospectives using our standard Scrum cadence
- Add unit and integration tests using JUnit 5 and Testcontainers, with mentorship on what to cover and why
- Debug production issues in our staging and lower environments, learning to read logs, traces, and metrics in Datadog
- Write clear pull request descriptions and respond constructively to code review feedback
- Document your work in our internal Confluence space so the rest of the team can build on it

What You Bring (Requirements)
- Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science, Software Engineering, or a related field, OR equivalent practical experience such as a completed coding bootcamp plus a portfolio of substantial projects
- Working knowledge of at least one object-oriented language — Java, C#, or similar — from coursework, internships, or personal projects
- Familiarity with basic data structures (arrays, lists, maps, trees) and when to use them
- Understanding of SQL fundamentals: SELECT, JOIN, GROUP BY, and basic normalization
- Exposure to version control with Git, including branching and pull requests
- Comfort reading and writing technical documentation in English
- Eagerness to learn, ask questions, and accept feedback as part of professional growth
- Legal authorization to work in the United States

Nice to Have
- Coursework or project experience with REST APIs and HTTP basics
- Familiarity with any cloud platform — AWS, GCP, or Azure — even at the free-tier level
- Exposure to Docker, Kubernetes, or container concepts through a tutorial or side project
- An open-source contribution, hackathon project, or personal GitHub repo you can show us

What We Offer
- Base salary of $68,000–$78,000 depending on experience, plus a discretionary annual bonus
- Full medical, dental, and vision coverage with 100% of employee premiums paid
- 4 weeks of PTO plus 11 company holidays
- $1,500 annual learning budget for books, courses, and conferences
- A defined mentorship pairing for your first year and a quarterly career development conversation
