Job Description: Senior Machine Learning Engineer

Company: Lattice Analytics, Inc.
Location: Remote (US-based); preference for candidates in Boston, MA or Austin, TX
Team: Applied ML — Forecasting Platform
Employment Type: Full-time
Salary Range: $165,000 – $210,000 base + equity + benefits
Visa Sponsorship: Available (H-1B transfer and O-1 considered)

About Lattice Analytics
Lattice Analytics is a 140-person Series C startup building demand-forecasting and inventory-optimization software for mid-market retailers and CPG brands. Our platform processes over 6 billion SKU-location-day forecasts per month for customers like Thrive Market, Bombas, and regional grocery chains. Founded in 2019 by former MIT CSAIL researchers and a ex-Stitch Fix engineering lead, we are backed by Andreessen Horowitz and Bain Capital Ventures. We are a remote-first team with optional offices in Boston and Austin, and we meet in person twice a year for company offsites.

The Role
We are hiring a Senior Machine Learning Engineer to join our Forecasting Platform team. You will own the end-to-end lifecycle of production forecasting models — from research and prototyping through deployment, monitoring, and continuous improvement. Our current stack runs on AWS (EKS, SageMaker, Airflow) with PyTorch and XGBoost serving ensembles behind a custom feature store. You will work closely with three other ML engineers, two ML researchers, and a platform team responsible for the underlying data and serving infrastructure. This is a hands-on IC role with significant technical autonomy and a clear path to Staff-level scope within 12–18 months for the right candidate.

Responsibilities
- Design, train, and evaluate forecasting models for retail demand, including transformer-based, gradient-boosted, and hierarchical reconciliation approaches.
- Own the production deployment of ML models on AWS SageMaker and Kubernetes, including CI/CD pipelines, canary releases, and automated rollback.
- Build and maintain monitoring, drift detection, and retraining systems for 40+ models in production.
- Collaborate with data engineering to define and evolve the feature store, with a focus on time-series feature correctness and backfill semantics.
- Partner with product and customer-facing teams to translate business requirements into modeling decisions and communicate trade-offs to non-technical stakeholders.
- Contribute to ML platform improvements such as experiment tracking (MLflow), model registry hygiene, and cost optimization of inference workloads.
- Participate in code reviews, design reviews, and incident response for ML-related production issues.
- Mentor junior engineers and help shape team best practices around reproducibility, evaluation rigor, and productionization hygiene.

Requirements
- 4+ years of professional experience building and deploying machine learning systems in production.
- Strong proficiency in Python and deep familiarity with at least one major deep learning framework (PyTorch preferred; TensorFlow acceptable).
- Hands-on experience with MLOps tooling such as MLflow, Kubeflow, SageMaker, Vertex AI, or equivalent in-house platforms.
- Solid understanding of SQL, data modeling, and working with large-scale time-series or tabular datasets.
- Experience with cloud infrastructure on AWS, GCP, or Azure (we primarily use AWS — EKS, S3, SageMaker, Airflow, Terraform).
- Familiarity with experiment design, statistical evaluation, and the practical pitfalls of A/B testing ML systems.
- Working knowledge of Docker and container orchestration (Kubernetes or ECS).
- Bachelor's or Master's degree in Computer Science, Statistics, Applied Mathematics, or a related quantitative field, or equivalent industry experience.

Nice to Have
- Experience with demand forecasting, supply chain, retail, or inventory optimization problems.
- Familiarity with hierarchical time-series reconciliation methods (e.g., MinT, BU, TD methods) or probabilistic forecasting libraries such as GluonTS, Prophet, or NeuralForecast.
- Contributions to open-source ML or MLOps projects.
- Experience with streaming data systems (Kafka, Kinesis, or Pub/Sub) and real-time feature engineering.
- Publications at venues such as NeurIPS, ICML, KDD, or AAAI, or equivalent applied research experience.
